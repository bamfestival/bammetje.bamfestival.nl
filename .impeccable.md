## Design Context

### Users
Primary visitors are people from Hengelo and the wider Twente region who want quick, reliable information about Bammetje 2026. Their main job is to understand what this mini-edition is, feel the BAM energy immediately, and decide to come because it is free, local, and easy to join.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel playful, warm, and energetic. The tone is welcoming and lively rather than exclusive or overly polished. Emotionally, the interface should create anticipation, local pride, and a sense that this is a compact festival with real character rather than a stripped-down fallback event.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a joyful, modern festival look with the BAM poster palette as the core system: burgundy, deep red, orange, yellow, and near-black, balanced with light surfaces so the site does not become too dark. The visual direction should feel scroll-based, fluid, and expressive, with bold headings, rounded shapes, layered gradients, and subtle motion. Support both light and dark contexts, but keep the overall experience brightened by warm highlights and sufficient contrast.

Reference cues already present in the repository should guide the work:
- warm festival gradients
- big display typography
- premium but approachable dark surfaces
- playful, non-corporate layout choices

Anti-direction:
- do not make it feel corporate
- do not make it feel overly dark or heavy
- do not make it look like a generic event template

### Design Principles
1. Lead with instant clarity. Visitors should understand within seconds that Bammetje is a free mini-edition of BAM! Festival in Hengelo/Twente.
2. Keep the vibe joyful and local. The site should feel like a lively community festival, not a formal institutional campaign.
3. Use bold visuals with restraint. Strong gradients, stickers, blobs, and expressive typography are welcome, but readability and hierarchy stay in control.
4. Stay accessible by default. WCAG 2.2 AA, visible focus states, semantic structure, keyboard access, reduced-motion support, strong contrast, and scalable typography are non-negotiable.
5. Design mobile-first and folder-safe. Layouts must work from small screens upward, assets must resolve correctly from subfolders, and performance should stay lean with modern image formats.
